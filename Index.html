The life of Jesus

The events preceding and leading up to His death

"for God so loved the world, that He gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have everlasting life." John 3:16

Dear God, as I attempt to create this tribute page in honour of your memory, I realize that you are not a memory but in actual fact still a reality. I ask you to please lead me and guide me through this adventure as I attempt to understand, but also explain your life on earth and the meaning thereof. Please help me to understand your scripture and interpret it correctly. Lord I ask you to please prepare my heart and mind, but also the hearts and minds of every person reading this to accept your message into our hearts and apply it in our lives. As can be derived from the title of the tribute page. The "person" my tribute page will be about is Jesus Christ.

For so many Christians Jesus is the core of the bible. Jesus have been described as the Son of God, the Saviour of the world, the Messiah. But who was He really, what was His life like, what were the vents preceding His death and waht does that mean for the human race? I will attempt to answer some of these questions and identify some of the "main" events in the life of Jesus as depicted in the scriptures.

  • The birth of Jesus prophesied
    • In the old testament we can see that the birth of Jesus was foretold long before His actual birth. "therefore the Lord Himself shall give you a sign; behold a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son, and shall call His name Immannuel." Isaiah 7:14

  • Mary visited by an angel
    • Now Mary, who was the mother of Jesus, was visited by an angel with the name of Gabriel. Gabriel said to Mary that she would conceive a son, as can be seen in Luke 1:31 "And, behold, thou shalt conceive in thy womb, and bring forth a son, and shalt call His name Jesus."

  • Mary found to be with child
    • Mary was betrothed (similar to being engaged) to Joseph. According to Jewish custom the couple my not have been intimate as of yet, but Mary was found to be pregnant. This can be seen in Matthew 1:18

  • An angel appears to Joseph in a dream
    • According to Jewish civil law, Joseph was in his full right to divorce from Mary, because of her apparent "unfaithfulness." While Joseph was thinking about all these things an angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream convincing him to marry Mary because what have happened to her is of the Holy Spirit. The baby was going to be a boy and were to be named "Jesus", which means "the Lord saves." Matthew 1:21

  • The birth of Jesus
    • Angels of the Lord appeared to shepherds whilest they were keeping watch over their flock. The angels announced to the shepherds that Jesus have been born in Betlehem. Luke 2:15-16

  • Mary and Joseph took Jesus to the temple
    • As was the Jewish custom, every male child was to be presented to God and a sacrifice was to be offered. Luke 2:22-24

  • Joseph escapes to Egypt with Mary and Jesus
    • Herod who was King of Israel heard that the King of the Jews were born and sought to kill Him because he didnt want the Jews to unite around a religious figure. An angel of the Lord appeared to Joseph in a dream to warn him of Herod and told him to take Mary and Jesus and to go to Egypt. Matthew 2:13-15

  • Return from Egypt
    • When Herod died Joseph took Mary and Jesus and returned to Nazareth. Matthew 2:19-21

  • Jesus was not an only child
    • Jesus was not an only child, He had brothers and sisters. Mark 6:3

  • Jesus is baptized
    • John the baptist baptized Jesus and the Holy Spirit descended upon Him. Luke 3:21-22

  • Jesus begins His ministry
    • Jesus began his ministry at about thirty years of age. Luke 3:23

  • satan tempts Jesus in the wilderness
    • Jesus filled with the Holy Spirit was led into the wilderness by the spirit to be tempted by the devil for fourty days. Luke 4:1-2

  • Jesus ministry in Galilee
    • Luke 4:14-15

  • Wonders performed by Jesus
    • Jesus went into the house of Simon, where Simon's mother in law was sick with fever. Jesus went to her and healed her. Luke 4:38-39 Jesus was approached by a man full of leprosy, and the man asked Jesus to heal him, if he was willing and Jesus healed him. Luke 5:12-14

  • Jesus has the last supper with His disciples
    • Matthew 26:26-29

  • Jesus pray in Gethsemane
    • Jesus was sorrowful when He thought of the events that was about to take place. Not only was Jesus going to experience physical pain, but He who have always been in perfect union with God will experience for the first time separation from Him. In Jesus' "humanity" He struggled to face the task which He was faced with and pleaded with His Father to let it pass Him. Although Jesus struggled with the task He did not want His will to be done, but the will of the Father. Matthew 26:36-45

  • Jesus betrayed and arrested
    • After Jesus had prayed in the garden of Gethsemane, judas who was one of Jesus' disciples betrayed Him by bringing a lot of people with him to arrest Jesus. judas gave them a sign saying "whosoever I shall kiss, that same is He: hold Him fast." Matthew 26:47-50

  • Jesus and the crown of thorns
    • A group of soldiers took Jesus and stripped His clothes of His body and dressed Him with a red robe. They then put a crown made of thorns on His head and handed Him a reed and mocked Him saying: hail, King of the Jews! Matthew 27:27-29

  • Jesus crucified
    • They took Jesus to a place called Golgotha "a place of a skull." There the soldiers crucified Him and took His garments and divided it between them by casting lots. Matthew 27:35

  • The death of Jesus
    • Darkness filled the land for about 3 hours from 6 to 9 and when it was the 9th hour, Jesus cried with a loud voice "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" Jesus cried the same just before He died. When Jesus died the veil of the temple tore in half and bodies started raising from the dead. A centurion and some of the people that was with him started to believe that Jesus was the Son of God after seeing all these things. Matthew 24:45-54

  • The burial of Jesus
    • A rich man with the name of Joseph went to Pilate to ask him for the body of Jesus. When he had received the body he wrapped it in linen and laid it down in a tomb. Matthew 27:57-60

  • Jesus resurrected
    • After the sabbath Mary Magdalene and the other Mary went to look at the tomb where Jesus was buried. There was an earthquake as one of the Lord's angels came down from heaven to go to the tomb. He rolled away the stone and sat on it. The angel told the two Mary's that Jesus was not in the grave anymore but that He has risen. He instructed them to go and tell the disciples of what has happened. The women left to tell the disciples all they have seen and heard and while on the road to go tell the disciples suddenly Jesus appeared to them, they fell to their knees and started to worship Him and He instructed them to tell the disciples to meet Him in Galilee. Matthew 28:1-10

  • On the road to Emmaus
    • Two of the disciples were traveling from Jerusalem to Emmaus when Jesus appeared to them, but they did not recognize Him. Luke 24:13-16 When they have arrived at the village, they convinced Jesus to stay with them as it was almost evening. While they were preparing to eat, Jesus gave thanks. He broke the bread, gave it to them and their eyes were opened and they recognized Him, then He dissapeared. They got up and returned to the other disciples who were still in Jerusalem and told them what have happened. Luke 24:28-35

  • Jesus appears to the disciples
    • As they were still telling the rest of the disciples about what happened, Jesus appeared to them, standing in their midst. The disciples were frightened and filled with fear, because they thought it was a ghost, but Jesus showed them His hands and feet so that they can see that it is really Him. Jesus told them that repentance and forgiveness of sins will be preached all over the world and that they will receive power from heaven. Luke 24:36-49

  • The ascension
    • Jesus led the disciples out to an area near Bethany, lifted up His hands and blessed them. While blessing them He dissapeared and was taken up into heaven. Luke 24:50-51

In conclusion:

Now why did Jesus have to die? Well, because all humans are sinners as can be seen in Romans 3:23 and our due penalty for sin is death. Romans 6:23 Now if God loves us why does He want us to go to hell? Well, the answer to that is He doesn't. God is a fair and just God, even though He loves us He can't let us escape the justice we deserve. Almost like a fair and just judge who needs to deliver a verdict on a case. So what can we do? Thats just it, it have been done a long time ago when God sent His only Son Jesus to die for our sins. John 3:16 The only way we can escape the penalty of death is to believe in Jesus.
